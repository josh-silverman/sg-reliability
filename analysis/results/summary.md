Generated by `scripts/run_analysis.py` (seed 42; rerun-verified identical).

All numbers below trace to the CSVs in this directory.

## Q2 — Stabilization (the headline)

Rounds needed for split-half reliability to reach R = 0.5 (= fitted k) and
R = 0.7, bootstrap 95% CIs (B = 1000):

| category | R=0.5 | CI | R=0.7 | CI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SG: off the tee | **8.7** | [7.9, 9.7] | 20.3 | [18.5, 22.5] |
| SG: approach | **29.6** | [25.7, 34.3] | 69.2* | [60.0, 80.1] |
| SG: around green | **39.2** | [35.5, 43.9] | 91.5* | [82.8, 102.5] |
| SG: putting | **47.6** | [42.1, 53.6] | 111.1* | [98.2, 125.1] |
| SG: total | 23.3 | [20.2, 27.2] | 54.4 | [47.2, 63.4] |

\* extrapolated beyond the n = 60 grid via the fitted Spearman-Brown form.

**The pre-registered expectation (approach fastest) was wrong: driving is
by far the most reliable category** — R = 0.5 in ~9 rounds, ~5.5x faster
than putting. Putting needs roughly two full seasons of tracked rounds to
hit R = 0.7.

## Q4 — Shrinkage and prediction

Weight to put on a 12-round sample (n/(n+k)); the rest regresses to the mean:
OTT **58%**, total 34%, APP 29%, ARG 23%, PUTT **20%**. A "hot putter" over
12 rounds is ~80% mean-reversion.

Predicting next-season total SG from season-t category means:

| category | univariate r | std. beta |
|---|---|---|
| sg_ott | 0.43 | 0.42 |
| sg_app | 0.40 | 0.29 |
| sg_arg | 0.26 | 0.23 |
| sg_putt | 0.16 | 0.26 |

Driving is the best single predictor of future total SG. Putting's
univariate r (0.16) understates its conditional contribution (beta 0.26)
because putting means are noisy and nearly uncorrelated with the other
categories.

## Sensitivity (documented, not pre-registered)

The primary R(n) grid uses whoever has ≥ 2n rounds, so the player pool
shrinks and homogenizes as n grows (this also explains the slight
non-monotonicity at n = 50→60). Re-estimating the whole curve on the fixed
pool of 386 player-windows with ≥ 120 rounds gives larger k everywhere
(OTT ~13, APP ~43, ARG ~45, PUTT ~49): among tour regulars there is less
true skill spread to detect, especially for approach. The OTT-fastest /
PUTT-slowest ordering is unchanged, but "how many rounds" always depends on
the reference population — worth a paragraph in the write-up.
